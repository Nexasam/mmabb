# MMAB Consulting — Screen Recording Instructions

Each step shows what to do on screen, followed immediately by what to say.

---

## Video 1 — Welcome to MMAB Consulting (~45 sec)

**DO:** Open the home page (`/`). Show the hero section with the brand gradient and headline.



**SAY:** "MMAB Consulting is a training platform built for healthcare professionals in paediatric complex care."




**DO:** Slowly scroll down past the stats bar — 500+ Professionals Trained, 3-Day Intensive Programme, 21 hrs CPD Accredited, UK-Wide Delivery — then through the six service cards.

**SAY:** "From the public site you can explore training areas, read real testimonials, and see case studies with measurable outcomes."




**DO:** Continue scrolling to the testimonials section and pause on one case study card showing an outcome like "94% improvement in protocol adherence".

**SAY:** "When you're ready, you can apply for a course directly from the site — no account needed."

---

## Video 2 — Browsing & Applying for a Course (~60 sec)

**DO:** Navigate to `/courses`. Let the page load. Hover over the Paediatric Complex Care course card — show the coloured top band, duration badge, location badge, and CPD badge.


**SAY:** "Head to the Courses page to see all available programmes. Each card shows the title, description, duration, location, and CPD badge."

**DO:** Click the **View Details** button on the Paediatric Complex Care course. Scroll through the detail page — overview text, learning objectives checklist, day-by-day curriculum, and the sticky sidebar showing the price and Apply Now button.
**SAY:** "Click View Details for the full course page — overview, learning objectives, day-by-day curriculum, and accreditation info."

**DO:** Click **Apply Now** in the sticky sidebar. Fill in the form: Full name, Email address, Phone number, and the Professional background textarea. Click **Submit Application**. Show the green success toast in the corner.
**SAY:** "When you're ready, hit Apply Now. Fill in your name, email, phone, and a short professional background. Submit and you're done — the team will review your application."

---

## Video 3 — Creating an Account & Your Dashboard (~50 sec)

**DO:** Navigate to `/register`. Show the two-panel layout — brand panel on the left, form on the right. Fill in Full name, Email address, Password, and Confirm password fields.
**SAY:** "To access materials and assessments you need an account. Registration takes seconds — name, email, and password."

**DO:** Click **Create my account**. Show the email verification prompt. Log in and land on `/dashboard`. Point at the "Welcome back, [Name]" hero banner.
**SAY:** "Once your email is verified, you land on your dashboard. It greets you by name and shows your training cards at a glance."

**DO:** Hover over the Course Materials card showing "Materials become available once your application is approved" and the Assessments card showing the same message. Then switch to an account with an approved application — show both cards in green with "Access Materials" and "View Assessments" links.
**SAY:** "Course Materials and Assessments are locked until your application is approved. Once approved, they turn green and link straight to your content."

---

## Video 4 — Admin: Reviewing Applications (~60 sec)

**DO:** Log in as admin. Navigate to `/admin/applications`. Show the four stat tab cards — All, Pending, Approved, Rejected — each with a count. Click through them to show the table filtering.
**SAY:** "Admins manage everything from a dedicated panel. On the Applications page, four tabs filter by status — All, Pending, Approved, and Rejected — each with a live count."

**DO:** Click the **Update** button on a pending application row. Show the dialog — point at the applicant info card with their initials avatar, name, email, and course title.
**SAY:** "Click Update on any row to open the review dialog. You can see the applicant's name, email, and course at a glance."

**DO:** Open the **New Status** dropdown and select **Approved**. Type a short note in the Notes textarea (e.g. "Welcome to the programme"). Click **Save Changes**. Show the status badge on the row update to green "Approved".
**SAY:** "Change the status to Approved or Rejected, optionally leave a note — rejected applicants will see that note — then hit Save Changes. The page updates instantly."

---

## Video 5 — Admin: Uploading Course Materials (~50 sec)

**DO:** Navigate to `/admin/materials`. Show the Course Materials page with course cards. Each card has a coloured header showing the course title and a file count badge.
**SAY:** "Course materials are managed from the Materials page. Each active course is listed with its uploaded PDFs."

**DO:** Click **Upload Material** in the top right. The dialog opens with a purple gradient header "Upload Course Material". Fill in: select a course from the dropdown, type a title (e.g. "Day 1 Workbook"), click the file input and select a PDF, set Sort Order to 1. Click **Upload Material**.
**SAY:** "Click Upload Material, pick the course, give the file a title, upload the PDF — up to 50 MB — and set a sort order to control the sequence students see."

**DO:** Show the dialog close and the new file appearing in the course card list with a red PDF icon, the file name, and file size.
**SAY:** "Files are stored privately. Students can never access them via a direct URL — every download is checked by the backend first."

## Video 6 — Student: Accessing Course Materials (~40 sec)

**DO:** Log in as a student with an approved application. Navigate to `/my-materials`. Show the Course Materials page — a course card with a brand-coloured header and the list of PDFs underneath.
**SAY:** "Once your application is approved, the My Materials page shows all PDFs for your course."

**DO:** Click the **View** button (with the eye icon) next to a PDF. Show the full-screen PDF viewer loading in an iframe with a Download button in the header.
**SAY:** "Click View to open a full-screen PDF viewer streamed securely from the server. Use the Download button if you want a local copy."

---

## Video 7 — Admin: Building an Assessment (~75 sec)

**DO:** Navigate to `/admin/assessments`. Show the Assessments page — assessments grouped by course, each row showing text/MCQ counts, time limit, and submission count. Click **New Assessment** in the top right.
**SAY:** "Assessments are built in the admin panel. Click New Assessment, pick a course, write a title, add optional instructions, and set a time limit."

**DO:** In the "Create Assessment" dialog: select a course, type a title (e.g. "End of Module Assessment"), type short instructions, set Time Limit to 30. Then click **Add Question**. Type a question, set marks to 5, leave type as **Text**.
**SAY:** "Then add your questions. Each one has a text field, a marks value, and a type — free text or multiple choice."

**DO:** Click **Add Question** again. Type a second question, set marks to 5, change the type dropdown to **MCQ**. Show the "Answer options — tick the correct one" panel appear. Add three options. Click the radio button next to the correct one.
**SAY:** "For MCQ questions, a second panel appears where you add answer options and tick the correct one. You can have as many options as you need."

**DO:** Point at the "Total: 10 marks" label in the Questions section header. Click **Create Assessment**.
**SAY:** "A running mark total updates as you build. Save and the assessment is live immediately."

## Video 8 — Student: Taking a Timed Assessment (~75 sec)

**DO:** Log in as student. Navigate to `/my-assessments`. Show the assessment cards — point at the status badge (Not started), the time limit, and the question count on a card.
**SAY:** "Students find their assessments at My Assessments. Each card shows the title, time limit, question count, and current status."

**DO:** Click **Start Assessment** at the bottom of a card. Show the sticky timer bar at the top — point at the "[X]/[Y] answered" count on the left and the countdown clock on the right.
**SAY:** "Click Start Assessment. A sticky bar at the top tracks how many questions you've answered and counts down the time. When five minutes remain, it turns red."

**DO:** Scroll to a text question. Click into the textarea and type a short answer. Scroll to an MCQ question. Click one of the radio option labels — show it highlight in the brand colour. If there's an optional question, point at its amber border and the star icon with "Optional" label.
**SAY:** "Text questions get a resizable textarea. MCQ questions show styled radio buttons — selecting an option highlights it. Optional questions have an amber border and a star icon."

**DO:** Click **Submit Assessment** in the fixed bottom bar. Show the confirmation dialog with the AlertTriangle icon — point at the answered count. Click **Yes, Submit**.
**SAY:** "When you're ready, click Submit Assessment. A confirmation dialog tells you how many questions you've answered. Confirm and the submission is locked."

---

## Video 9 — Admin: Marking a Submission (~75 sec)

**DO:** Navigate to `/admin/assessments`. Click the eye icon (View submissions) on an assessment row. Show the submissions page — three stat cards at the top: Submissions, Marked, Pending Mark. Show the table with student names, submitted dates, and status badges.
**SAY:** "The Submissions page shows everyone who has attempted an assessment — their status, submission date, and score if already marked."

**DO:** Click **Mark** on a row showing "Needs marking". Show the "Mark Submission" dialog — point at the student info card (name, email) and the live score preview showing "0%" in the top right of that card.
**SAY:** "Click Mark to open the marking dialog. Each question shows the student's answer with a mark input capped at the question's maximum."

**DO:** Type a mark into the first question's number input. Show the colour bar next to it change (green if high, amber if mid, red if low). Type a mark into the second question. Show the percentage in the student info card update in real time.
**SAY:** "A colour-coded bar gives instant feedback — green for strong, amber for partial, red for low. The live score preview at the top updates as you type."

**DO:** Scroll to the Overall Feedback textarea. Type a short note (e.g. "Good understanding of the core concepts"). Point at the footer showing "Calculated score: X% (Y/Z marks)". Click **Save Marks**.
**SAY:** "Add overall feedback in the notes field — the student will see this on their result page. Click Save Marks and marking is complete."

## Video 10 — Student: Viewing Your Result (~45 sec)

**DO:** Log in as student. Navigate to `/my-assessments`. Find a card with a green "X%" status badge. Click **View Result** at the bottom of the card.
**SAY:** "Once marked, the result page shows a gradient banner with your final score as a large percentage, the submission date, and the marker's notes."

**DO:** Go back and find a card with a blue "Awaiting mark" badge. Click **View Result** on it. Show the grey gradient banner with the "Awaiting mark" badge and the amber info box: "Your submission is being reviewed."
**SAY:** "If your assessment is still being reviewed, the banner shows Awaiting Mark with a message explaining what to expect."

**DO:** Go back to the marked result. Scroll down through the "Your Answers" section showing each question and the student's written answer. End on the **Back to Assessments** button.
**SAY:** "Either way, all your answers are listed below so you can review what you wrote."

## General Recording Tips

- Browser zoom: **90%**
- Resolution: **1920×1080** minimum
- Move the cursor slowly and deliberately — fast mouse movement is hard to follow
- Pause on each DO step for 1–2 seconds before speaking
- Pre-seed the database with sample data before recording Videos 4, 7, and 9 so you're not working with empty states
